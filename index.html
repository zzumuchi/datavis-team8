Introduction

The steam engine, the computer, and now AI —
technology has always reshaped work.

From agriculture to manufacturing, from manufacturing to services. Every time technology shifted, so did where people worked. AI isn't the end of this story — it's the next turning point.

The Wrong Question

And now, the first worry on everyone's mind is the same.
"Is my job going to disappear?"

But that might be the wrong question. Jobs don't disappear all at once. They transform — task by task. The question is: which parts of work are actually changing?

Part 1

A Job Is a Bundle of Tasks

Take a software engineer — their work includes writing code, reviewing code, debugging, analyzing requirements, and communicating with the team. A single job title is shorthand for dozens of interwoven activities.

Source: O*NET 25.0 Database, Work Activities (Importance ratings), U.S. Department of Labor

But the core tasks differ dramatically from job to job.

Part 2

Does high AI usage mean high replacement risk?

Not necessarily. AI intervenes in two distinct ways: replacing humans entirely (automation), or working alongside them (augmentation). The chart below shows which tasks fall into each category.

◀ Automation Augmentation ▶

Source: Anthropic Economic Index (Feb 2025) AI usage share by task × O*NET occupation-level automation probability (estimated)

In reality, augmentation is far more common.

In far more cases than most people expect, AI isn't taking over human work — it's working right beside us. The augmentation bars extend consistently further than automation bars.

Part 3 — The Real Numbers

So what does the actual employment data say?

It's worth checking whether real hiring trends match what we'd expect — given everything the media has claimed about AI displacing workers.

AI Adoption vs Employment

AI adoption has climbed steeply. But the overall employment line has barely budged.

Whole jobs disappearing en masse — it simply hasn't happened.

So What Should We Ask?

So the question needs to change.

Not "Will my job disappear?" — but "Which tasks inside my job are changing, and how?"

The goal isn't to predict what AI will take.
It's to know where you should be investing your time — before someone else decides for you.
